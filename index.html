It's safe, and it works

Evidence shows fecal transplantation to be safe and effective.

In a thorough review of fecal transplantation therapy by Landy et al, the authors report:

"No studies of faecal transplantation report any adverse events related to the procedure. "
"Faecal transplantation for C. difficile has been demonstrated to be effective in 145/166 (87%) patients."
"For ulcerative colitis, of eight patients reported, all have responded and have remained in remission from 6 months to 13 years." [1]
According to a similar review by Smits et al,
"In a case series of 3 patients with chronic constipation, FMT [Fecal Microbiota Transplantation] greatly reduced constipation during the month after FMT; all patients defecated daily (or every other day) without the assistance of laxatives." [2]
Borody et al,
"Other disease states that are closely linked to the GI microbiota, such as obesity, metabolic syndrome, and diabetes mellitus, may potentially be treated by FMT in the future. A recent pilot study assessed the effects of FMT from lean healthy donors to individuals with metabolic syndrome. Six weeks post-FMT, improved insulin sensitivity was noted in the 9 recipients, along with intestinal microbiota changes characterized by increased butyrate-producing bacteria." [3]
